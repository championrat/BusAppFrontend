Driver Guidelines

Welcome to the driver guidelines page. As a driver, you are expected to follow the rules and guidelines set by our company to ensure the safety of passengers, other drivers, and yourself. Please read the following guidelines carefully.

General Rules

  • Safety First: Always prioritize safety, both for yourself and for your passengers. Follow all road safety rules, including wearing your seatbelt at all times.
  • Respect the Speed Limits: Adhere to all posted speed limits and drive according to the road and weather conditions.
  • Maintain Cleanliness: Keep your bus clean and comfortable for passengers. Report any damage or maintenance needs immediately.
  • Be Punctual: Arrive on time for your scheduled trips. Delays should be communicated promptly to the company or passengers when necessary.
  • Follow Company Procedures: Abide by company policies related to bookings, passenger handling, and handling of bus incidents.

Passenger Interaction

  • Professional Behavior: Maintain a polite and professional attitude at all times when interacting with passengers.
  • Provide Assistance: Help passengers with their luggage, assist elderly or disabled passengers, and make their journey comfortable.
  • Handle Disruptive Passengers: If a passenger is disruptive or unruly, contact your supervisor or the authorities as per company policy.

Bus Operation

  • Vehicle Inspection: Before each trip, perform a pre-trip inspection of the bus to ensure it is in good working order. Report any issues to the maintenance team immediately.
  • Fueling: Make sure to refuel the bus when it is low on fuel. Follow company procedures for refueling and report any issues with fuel usage.
  • Emergency Procedures: Familiarize yourself with the emergency procedures, including how to handle breakdowns, accidents, and emergencies involving passengers.

Health and Safety

  • Drive Sober: Never drive under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or any substances that impair your ability to drive.
  • Rest Periods: Take regular rest breaks as required to avoid fatigue. Driving while tired can be as dangerous as driving under the influence.
  • COVID-19 Guidelines: If applicable, follow all necessary health protocols to ensure the safety of yourself and your passengers, including mask-wearing and sanitization procedures.

By following these guidelines, you will help ensure a safe, enjoyable, and professional driving experience for both you and your passengers. Thank you for being a responsible driver!
